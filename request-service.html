How It Works

Keep the request focused

Start with the event title, how many people you expect to need, a short description of the support request, and the date. Those four fields give staff the fastest read on what is needed.

You can still copy the draft text if you want a manual backup for email or a direct conversation.

What to Include

Start with these details.

The more complete the initial request, the faster leadership can assess support requirements and respond clearly.

Event Basics

Date and time

Include the event date, expected timeline, arrival window, and any setup or recovery period.

Location

Venue and access details

Share the exact location, access instructions, parking details, and any restrictions that affect movement.

Scale

Expected attendance

Provide an estimate of crowd size, activity level, and any higher-risk factors leadership should know about.

Point of Contact

Who will coordinate onsite

Include the primary requester, best contact method, and who the team should meet on the day of the event.

Lead Time

Earlier requests are easier to support

Advance notice gives the club time to evaluate requirements, confirm availability, and coordinate the right internal staffing plan.

Coordination

Leadership will follow up directly

After a request is received, leadership can clarify scope, ask for missing details, and confirm whether support can be provided.
